School Experience
(In the field)

        When it comes to learning, nothing beats hands-on work. Experiencing workflow/work through actual field work is leaps and bounds more useful than just sitting in front of a textbook. Of course, aside from the field work that can be seen in my about me section and on my resume, I have been fortunate enough to be part of a program which also pushes its students to gain field experience. Here are some of my first-hand experiences in the field with my fellow classmates.
        First off, there were field trips. One trip that our class took was to SAP. At SAP, us students were given a tour of the facility along with a in depth description of what the company does. Many of the staff members explained their positions and roles, and got us curious. I found a couple important takeaways after going to be SAP. First off, extracurriculars are always good. Working outside of school, in any way possible, is always a benefit. Second, networking skills are key. You don't necessarily have to change yourself as a person, but you must be able to be open and communicate.
        Secondly, there are guest speakers. Civil engineers and guest speakers Dan Cherrier and Natalina Bernardi spoke to our Princples of Engineering class on civil engineering as a major in college and as a career. While giving they gave the presentation, I found out about the different licenses and requirements for being a civil engineer. Some things that piqued my interest were when both presenters said that one, you can get a high position from just a bachelor's degree, and two, civil engineering is a very difficult. I didn't expect this, as I always thought it wasn't that difficult of a major, but the more you know the more interesting things become.
